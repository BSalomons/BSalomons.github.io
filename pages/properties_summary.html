Properites & Listings Summary

There are two primary “buckets” of data as it relates to real estate transactions. They are related to each other but it is important to visualize them separately to ensure the proper data is being entered.

The first is Properties. The data associated with this object is the physical characteristics of the property; it is the attributes that don’t change without great expense. For example, it is the acreage of the lot; the square footage of a building; the building height; the zoning; its address; pictures and so on. Unless a redevelopment, subdivision, or renovation occurs, these attributes won’t change and once it is entered it doesn’t need to be entered again.

The second is Listings. The data associated with this object is the transactional information associated with the sale or lease of a Property. For example, it is the purchase price; the lease rate; the MLS number; the signage on the property; the status of the listing; the features and so on. It is a transaction that is occurring at a point in time and can occur again at any point in the future for the same Property.

A Property is the parent of a Listing as you can’t have a Listing without the underlying Property to lease or sell. Most of the fields that are displayed on the front end of the website are Listing fields.

Adding and editing Properties and Listings must be done in the Database and not in Salesforce. This is because it is a one-way sync wherein the Database updates Salesforce but not the other way. Therefore data entered into Salesforce will be overwritten once the record is updated in the Database.
